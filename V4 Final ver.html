Frequently Asked Questions

Why is there such a massive gap in borrowing power between new and established homes?
When a property is negatively geared, banks treat your anticipated tax refund as "usable cash" to help service your loan. Because the May 2026 budget removes this tax refund for established homes purchased after July 2027, your usable net income drops significantly in the eyes of the bank, leading to heavily reduced maximum loan approvals.
I already own an investment property. Will I lose my negative gearing benefits?
No. If you purchased an established investment property before the budget cut-off—7:30 PM AEST on 12 May 2026—your property is "grandfathered." You can continue to negatively gear this specific property against your salary for as long as you own it.
If I buy a 'New Build' to get negative gearing, do I lose the tax benefit if I refinance with a different bank?
No. The budget exemptions are tied to the physical property and the original contract date, not your loan provider. Refinancing an exempt property to get a better interest rate will retain its grandfathered tax status.
If I sell my grandfathered investment property to someone else, do they get to keep the negative gearing benefits?
No. Grandfathering status is strictly tied to the person or entity who held the contract prior to the May 2026 budget cut-off. If you sell the property, it is treated as an "established home" purchase for the new buyer, and they will not be able to negatively gear it against their salary.
Can I transfer my grandfathered property to my spouse or a family trust without losing the tax benefits?
No. Any change in legal ownership—including transferring a title to a spouse, child, or self-managed super fund—is considered a "new acquisition" by the ATO. The new owner will fall under the post-July 2027 rules and will lose the ability to negatively gear the established property against their day-to-day salary.
Can I buy an old house, do a 'knock-down rebuild', and claim it as a New Build?
No. The government specifically closed this loophole. The May 2026 budget explicitly states that replacing an older, free-standing house with a knock-down rebuild on an existing established block does not qualify for the new build exemptions.
If I buy an established home after July 2027, do my rental losses just disappear?
No, they aren't lost completely, but they are "quarantined." You can no longer use them to reduce the tax on your PAYG salary. Instead, you carry these losses forward to offset future rental profits, or use them to reduce your Capital Gains Tax when you sell.
Why do Specialist Lenders offer so much more borrowing capacity than the Big 4?
Specialist lenders assess risk differently. They often use lower internal assessment buffers compared to APRA-regulated standard banks, allowing them to stretch borrowing capacities further.
What happens if I buy an 'off-the-plan' apartment now, but it doesn't settle until 2028?
Because it is a brand-new build, it is entirely exempt from the new restrictions regardless of the settlement date. You will be able to negatively gear it against your salary once it is completed and tenanted.
Why does an Interest Only (IO) loan reduce my maximum borrowing capacity?
APRA regulations require banks to ensure you can afford the loan once the IO period ends and the loan reverts to Principal & Interest. If you have a 5-year IO period on a 30-year loan, the bank must compress the entire principal repayment into the remaining 25 years. This higher hypothetical monthly repayment reduces the maximum amount they can lend you.
